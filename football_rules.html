Football

Football is a team sport in which two teams play against each other to score their spherical ball in the other opponent's net (what we call a 'goal').

Football positions on the pitch:

  • Goalkeeper - The keeper stays in the net and protects it trying to stop and save balls from going in their goal.
  • Left/Right Fullback - positioned on the right/left side of the pitch and is a defending position.
  • Centre Back - Their role is to defend the central side of the pitch.
  • Left/Right Midfielder - These players can also be referred to as 'wingers' and they stay wide trying to pull the opponent’s defence to create space to achieve a goal.
  • Central Midfielder - Plays defensively and offensively, they are responsible for kicking the ball strategically to other players.
  • Striker - This is an offensive position and is positioned nearest to the opposing team’s goal. Their main job is to score a goal.
  • Attacking Midfielder - These players sit between the midfield and offensive line, they must have good dribbling skills. Their main job is to create scoring opportunities.

Football rules

  • Each match is 45 minutes long with two halves to allow for a 15-minute break in between.
  • No player (apart from the goalkeeper) should touch the ball if a player touches the ball the opposing team gets a penalty and is called ‘handball’.
  • A maximum of 11 players are allowed and a minimum of 7.
  • To score a goal the ball must be in the opposing teams net and must be fully in, meaning it has successfully crossed the line, the goal can be scored from any part of the body apart from your hands.
  • The football used must be 58-61cm and be a circular shape.
  • Players must wear protective gear including shin pads and the correct boots.
  • Each side is allowed a maximum of three substitutions each, in case any player isinjured or the game rolls into extra time.
  • The winning team is the one who scores the most goals. If a match results in both teams scoring the same, the match will end in a draw. If it’s a cup game extra time can be added which is an extra 30 minutes.
  • Each game must have a referee and two assistants. It’s the referee's job to make sure the game is being played fair, they can rule penalties, plus give yellow and red cards.
  • A yellow card is a warning, two of them would result in a red card and a player being taken off the pitch. If this is the case a player in this situation cannot be replaced.
  • If a ball goes out of the pitch (out of play) then it can be played as a ‘throw in’ in which a player throws the ball back into the game.
  • If a ball goes out of play on the baseline of an attacking player, they get a goal kick (e.g penalty) But if it comes from a defending player it’s a corner kick.

The Offside Rule in Football

Offside can be called when an attacking player is in front of the last defender when the pass is played through to them. The offside area is designed to discourage players from simply hanging around the opponent’s goal waiting for a pass. To be onside they must be placed behind the last defender when the ball is played to them. If the player is in front of that last defender then he is deemed to be offside and free kick to the defending team will be called.

Offside - Photo: KreedOn

A player cannot be caught offside in their own half. The goalkeeper does not count as a defender. If the ball is played backwards and the player is in front of the last defender then he is deemed to be not offside.

Basic Football Skills Needed to Play the game

To be able to play the game successfully there are a few basic football skills that need to be mastered to achieve the best result. Football players regularly practice these in their training to perfect and master them.

  • Dribbling skills - This is a vital skill that involves skillfully controlling the ball. This can firstly be taught by dribbling the ball around cones and build it up from there. Dribbling comes into practice throughout the game when passing the ball from player to player and dodging opponents.
  • Accuracy - This is crucial to learn when passing the ball in a skilful and tactical manner, to avoid the opposing team gaining control of the ball.
  • Spatial Awareness - This is important as it’s vital to stay to your correct area of the pitch and to successfully play your position correctly.
  • Communication Skills - Good listening and communication skills are another important skill as without them the game will not be successful. It’s important to listen to your teammates to tactfully play the game and get the best result.
  • Teamwork - It’s important that everyone playing the game knows and understands their role and responsibilities. It’s also vital for each player to value everyone’s efforts and come together as a team to achieve a team win.
  • Master these five basic football skills and you’ll stand a great chance of winning with a solid formed team.

Five Fun Football Facts

  • Football is believed to be the most-watched sport in the world.
  • Only America and Canada call the sport soccer.
  • It’s believed football originated from China in 476 B.C.
  • Neil Armstrong (the first man on the moon) reportedly wanted to take a football with him but sadly NASA didn’t permit this.
  • It’s estimated that players run around 9.65km each game, that’s roughly around 5 miles.
